## What it is

Aider is a terminal-based AI pair programmer that operates directly on your git
repository. You describe a change in natural language, Aider edits the files,
and each accepted change gets its own git commit. It's the most "git-native"
of the agent CLIs.

## What it does well

- **Git as the interaction model.** Every change is a commit you can review,
  revert, or rebase. There's no opaque "agent state" to lose track of — the
  repo history is the truth.
- **Repository-aware edits.** Aider builds a repo map so the model knows which
  files exist and roughly what's in them, even without loading them all into
  context.
- **Model-agnostic.** Works with any frontier API (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google,
  DeepSeek), local models via Ollama, or anything OpenAI-compatible.
- **Pure terminal.** No GUI, no editor lock-in. Lives wherever your shell lives.

## Trade-offs

- **No autocomplete.** Aider is for "describe a change, get a diff" — it
  doesn't replace tab completion or inline suggestions. Pair it with an editor
  for that.
- **You manage context.** You /add files to the chat explicitly. Powerful when
  you know what you're doing, occasionally tedious when you don't.
- **Terminal-only is a feature and a constraint.** No visual diff, no
  hover-over-symbol; you live in your terminal and your editor of choice.

## Who it's for

Developers who already think in commits and live in a terminal, anyone who
wants an AI workflow that survives switching between editors, and anyone who
wants to point their AI at a different model with one config change. Pair with
your editor of choice — Aider is deliberately editor-agnostic.
