## When to reach for Claude Fable 5

Claude Fable 5 is Anthropic's first publicly available **Mythos-class** model
(released June 9, 2026), and its most capable general-availability model to date.
It posts state-of-the-art results across nearly all tested benchmarks, with
standout strength in software engineering, knowledge work, vision, and scientific
research.

- The hardest autonomous, long-horizon coding and knowledge work.
- Tasks where you want maximum capability and are willing to pay the top tier.
- Agentic workflows needing deep, adaptive reasoning over a large context.

For the everyday 80%, **Claude Sonnet 4.6** remains the better-value default, and
**Opus 4.8** is a cheaper frontier option.

## Fable vs. Mythos

Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 are the **same underlying model**. The difference is
safety: Fable adds guardrails that intervene when a session enters sensitive
territory (cybersecurity, biology, chemistry, and some forms of model extraction).
These safeguards trigger in under ~5% of sessions on average; sensitive requests
can route to a Claude Opus 4.8 fallback.

## Specs & cost

- **Context:** 1M tokens by default, up to 128K output tokens per request.
- **Inputs:** text, images, and files; text output.
- **Thinking:** adaptive thinking only — use the `effort` parameter to control depth.
- **Pricing:** $10 / 1M input and $50 / 1M output.
- **Availability:** Claude API / Platform / Code, plus AWS, Google Cloud, and
  Microsoft Foundry (and GitHub Copilot).
