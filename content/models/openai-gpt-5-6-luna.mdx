## When to reach for GPT-5.6 Luna

Luna is the fast, cost-efficient tier of the GPT-5.6 family (GA July 9, 2026) —
the pick for high-volume, latency-sensitive workloads where per-token cost
compounds quickly.

- Classification, extraction, routing, and other well-scoped bulk tasks.
- Latency-sensitive UX where a frontier model would feel slow.
- Cost-dominated pipelines — Luna is the cheapest 5.6 tier by a wide margin.

Unusually for a "small" tier, Luna keeps the family's full ~1M-token context
window, which makes it a strong long-context bargain.

## Specs & cost

- **Context:** ~1.05M tokens, 128K max output (shared across the 5.6 family).
- **Pricing:** $1 / 1M input and $6 / 1M output. Cached-input reads are 90%
  cheaper; cache writes bill at 1.25× input.

> Benchmark scores intentionally omitted: published Luna numbers vary widely by
> reasoning-effort configuration (non-reasoning through xhigh), so there is no
> single stable figure to report — and OpenAI did not publish academic
> benchmarks at launch. We don't cherry-pick a config.
