## When to reach for Gemini 2.5 Pro

Gemini 2.5 Pro's defining feature is the 1M-token context window — and unlike many
"long context" models, it actually uses what's in there. If your workload involves
shoving a whole codebase, hours of transcripts, or many PDFs into a single prompt,
Gemini is the natural pick.

- Repository-scale code understanding and refactoring.
- Document analysis where the answer requires reasoning across hundreds of pages.
- Video understanding — Gemini natively accepts video input, not just frame extracts.

## Prompt patterns that work well

- **Structure long context.** Even with 1M tokens, ordering matters. Put the task at
  the top, the documents in the middle, and restate the task at the bottom.
- **Ask grounded questions.** "What does section 4.2 say about retries?" outperforms
  "Tell me about retries" when the input is large — the model has somewhere to anchor.
- **Use thinking tokens deliberately.** Gemini 2.5's reasoning modes trade latency
  for depth; toggle them based on whether the user is waiting interactively.

## Multimodal: video

Video is the most distinctive input modality here. Pass an MP4 or a YouTube URL and
ask questions across the timeline — Gemini will reason about what's on screen, what's
said, and how the two relate.
