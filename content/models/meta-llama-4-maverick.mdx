## When to reach for Llama 4 Maverick

Maverick is Meta's mid-tier Llama 4 variant — natively multimodal, mixture-of-experts
architecture, and crucially open weights. Pick it when you need to run inference on
your own infrastructure, fine-tune for a vertical use case, or avoid vendor lock-in
on a long-lived product.

- On-prem or VPC-only deployments where sending tokens to a US-hosted API is
  off the table.
- Domain fine-tuning where you're starting from a strong general checkpoint and
  adapting it with your own data.
- Workloads where you want to pin the model version and never have it silently
  updated underneath you.

## Hosted vs self-hosted

You can use Maverick three ways:

1. **Hosted inference providers** (Together, Fireworks, Groq, etc.). Cheapest path
   to "just call it." Pricing varies — typically lower per-token than frontier
   closed models, but check each provider.
2. **Cloud GPUs you rent** (A100/H100 instances on AWS/GCP). Reasonable for
   moderate throughput; you own the deployment.
3. **Your own hardware**. Best for steady-state high-volume workloads where the
   capex amortizes.

## Prompt patterns that work well

- **Don't over-prompt.** Llama 4 is more sensitive to wandering instructions than
  the closed frontier models. Be direct.
- **Use the chat template correctly.** The right system/user/assistant boundaries
  matter — most hosted providers handle this for you, but check when self-hosting.
- **Fine-tune for narrow tasks.** Maverick is general-purpose; for a specific
  vertical, even a small LoRA fine-tune is a meaningful quality bump.
